How NullVPN Works

How is NullVPN different from NordVPN, ExpressVPN, or ProtonVPN?

Consumer VPNs use protocols that Deep Packet Inspection (DPI) systems can detect and block — even when encrypted. They also rely on app stores (which governments can pressure to remove VPN apps) and run on well-known IP ranges that get blocklisted continuously.

NullVPN routes through the same port used by every bank, hospital, and e-commerce site globally. To a firewall or DPI system, your connection looks identical to secure web traffic. There is no VPN signature to detect or block. See the full comparison →

What do you mean it's "not a regular VPN"?

Regular VPNs encrypt your traffic but use recognisable protocols — OpenVPN, IKEv2, WireGuard. These have traffic patterns that Deep Packet Inspection can identify and block, even without reading your data.

NullVPN uses multiple independent encryption layers and routes through the same port that firewalls cannot block without taking down global banking and e-commerce. Your connection appears as normal encrypted secure browsing. Think of it this way: consumer VPNs try to hide that you're using a VPN. NullVPN doesn't look like a VPN at all.

How does the auto-failover work?

NullVPN uses a layered tunnel architecture. The primary connection uses an obfuscated protocol that is invisible to DPI. If this is blocked on a specific network, the app automatically and silently switches to a secondary route within seconds. You stay connected. You never notice the switch. This is why NullVPN keeps working even after consumer VPNs are blocked on the same network.

Why do consumer VPNs keep getting blocked?

Four main reasons: (1) Known IP ranges — major VPN providers have IPs listed publicly and governments blocklist them continuously. (2) Detectable protocols — OpenVPN, IKEv2 and WireGuard have traffic fingerprints that DPI systems identify. (3) Predictable ports — firewalls can simply block ports 1194, 500, and 4500 that VPNs use. (4) App store dependency — when Apple or Google remove a VPN app under government pressure, users instantly lose access. NullVPN is designed to avoid all four vulnerabilities.
